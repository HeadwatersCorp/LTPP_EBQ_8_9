## ğŸ¯ Project Background

The **PRRIP** aims to improve the reproductive success of **piping plovers** (*Charadrius melodus*) along the central Platte River. In recent years, particularly **2018â€“2019**, plover fledge ratios (chicks fledged per breeding pair) reached record lows. In response, stakeholders prioritized understanding:

- The extent to which **predation affects plover productivity**
- Whether **predator management practices** are effective at reducing those impacts

This work is structured around **Extension Big Questions 8 and 9** from the PRRIP Extension Science Plan (PRRIP 2022a).
